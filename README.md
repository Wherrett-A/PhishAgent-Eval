# Machina Persona: Evaluating the Efficacy of genAI Phishing Against Human Judgment and Modern Email Defenses.

![License](https://img.shields.io/badge/License-MIT-green.svg)
![Status](https://img.shields.io/badge/Status-Research_In_Progress-orange.svg)

## Overview
As Large Language Models (LLMs) evolve, the "traditional" markers of phishing—poor grammar, spelling errors, and awkward syntax—are disappearing. This project investigates the shift from Manual Phishing (human-authored) to Agentic Phishing (autonomous AI workflows).

Using a custom-built Simulated Email Sandbox, this study measures how human participants perceive the trustworthiness of emails generated by autonomous agents compared to verified historical phishing attacks. The project further evaluates the efficacy of modern, AI-integrated email defense heuristics (similar to Gmail/Outlook 2026) in flagging these "perfect" synthetic deceptions.

### Research Questions
- To what extent does agentic, LLM-generated phishing content achieve higher user trustworthiness scores compared to human-authored phishing samples from historical archives?
- What is the cost-to-success ratio of deploying an autonomous agentic phishing workflow versus traditional manual campaign creation for a bad actor?

  - Model: GLM-5
